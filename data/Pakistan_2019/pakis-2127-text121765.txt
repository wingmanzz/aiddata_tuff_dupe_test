Title: WAPDA signs PKR 52.5bn contract for Dasu hydroelectric power project

Snippet: The project will add 2,160MW or 12bn units per annum of electricity to the national grid

The World Bank is financing part of the project's construction

Body: 
WAPDA signed a PKR 52.5bn contract with a joint venture of General Electric Hydro China and Power China Zhongnan Engineering Corporation for the construction of the first stage of Dasu hydroelectric power project in Kohistan, a project that will add 2,160 megawatt (MW) of electricity to the national grid upon completion in 2024, media reported on Monday. The contract included preparing the design, supply and installation of six Francis turbines, generators, main transformers, and other related equipment.

The 4,320MW Dasu hydroelectric power project will be completed in two stages - each stage having a generation capacity of 2,160MW. The project's stage-I will contribute more than 12bn units per annum to the national grid. The World Bank is providing funds for the construction of the project's stage-I to the tune of USD 588.4mn whereas the remaining funds are being arranged by WAPDA from its own resources and with sovereign guarantee of the Pakistan government.
