Title: KP Forest Minister visits China Culture Centre

Snippet: PwrMinister for Forest, Environment and Wildlife Department Syed Mohammad Ishtiaq Urmar said Wednesday that Pakistan and China were enjoying friendly relations and Prime Minister Imran Khan's upcoming visit to China would further bring the two countries closer to each other.

He said that PTI-led government took pride in friendship with China which was a time-tested and had always supported Pakistan in critical hour.

Body: 
He expressed these views during his visit to Chinese culture center-China Window in Peshawar where he was taken round to different galleries in the center and briefed about various aspects of the Chinese culture.

The Minister was informed that the center aimed at strengthening people-to-people contact between the two countries besides knowing about each other's culture.

Speaking on the occasion, the minister said the provincial government accorded approval to set up Rashakai economic zone which would be prepared by the Chinese government, meeting all requirements of the modern era.

He said the provincial cabinet had also given formal approval to this mega project which would open new avenues of socio-economic development.

He also lauded the Chinese embassy for taking interest in social welfare projects in Khyber Pakhtunkwa and added that provincial government would extend all possible support in this regard.

He said the government extended invitation to the Chinese investors to come and invest in the province as this part could serve as gateway for export to Central Asian countries. The minister also announced another gallery and stall in connection with billion tree scheme.
